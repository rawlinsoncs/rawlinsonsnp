What Is the Student Nutrition Program?

The Rawlinson Student Nutrition Program (SNP) provides a daily snack to every student at the school.

Students can receive a snack regardless of whether a family has submitted an opt-in form.
This approach helps ensure all students are supported during the school day.

The program exists to:

  • Support student wellbeing
  • Promote equitable access to food
  • Reduce barriers for families

How the Program Works

The Student Nutrition Program is supported through:

  • Public funding
  • Food donations
  • Community support

Much of the program is run by volunteers, with support from school administration.

Because a significant portion of the food is donated and perishable:

  • Menus may change week to week
  • Substitutions are not always possible
  • Items are sometimes prioritized to reduce waste

The goal is consistency and equity, not perfection.

What Does the Snack Include?

Each snack day includes two components, following Student Nutrition Program guidelines:

1. Vegetables or fruit

2. One of the following:

  • Whole grain products
  • Protein foods
  • Milk or milk alternatives

Specific items vary depending on availability, seasonality, and food safety requirements.

Nutrition Standards

All snacks follow nutrition standards set by the TDSB and the City of Toronto.

These standards include guidance around:

  • Sugar and sodium limits
  • Whole grain ingredients
  • Appropriate food categories for student nutrition programs

Dietary Needs and Allergies

We take dietary needs and allergies seriously.

  • Students with allergies or medical dietary needs receive appropriate substitutions
  • Temporary dietary needs (for example, related to religious observance) can be accommodated when possible

Important notes:

  • Allergy or medical concerns must be communicated in writing through the school
  • Allergy concerns cannot be lifted based on verbal requests alone
  • If a student does not receive a substitution item, their dietary needs do not require one

If you have questions about your child’s dietary needs, please contact the school office.

How the Program Is Funded

The Student Nutrition Program is supported through:

  • Public funding
  • Food donations
  • Community contributions

Donated food often includes fresh, perishable items such as fruit and dairy.
Additional items may be purchased to supplement donations when needed.

How Families Can Help

Families can support the program by:

  • Volunteering time when possible
  • Making financial contributions (Receipts available for donations over $10)
  • Sharing accurate information within the community
  • Expressing appreciation for volunteers and staff

Every form of support helps keep the program running smoothly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can families request specific snack items?

Families are welcome to share feedback, but specific items cannot be guaranteed.
Availability, safety requirements, and nutrition guidelines determine what can be served.

Why don’t we serve certain foods?

The school does not have a food preparation kitchen or food handling certifications.

For safety reasons, this limits what foods can be served through the program.

Who runs the Student Nutrition Program?

With the exception of school administration support, the program is run primarily by volunteers, including:

  • Program coordination
  • Snack packing and distribution
  • Managing dietary substitutions

Who should families contact with questions?

Please contact the school office for questions related to:

  • Dietary needs
  • Allergies
  • Program participation

This helps ensure information is shared safely and consistently.
