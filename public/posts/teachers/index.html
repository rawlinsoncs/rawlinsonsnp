Teacher Quick Start

If you only read one section, read this.

Please reach out to the Snack Program if:

  • You notice a student has a dietary change due to an allergy, medical reason, or religious observance.
  • Your class requires a larger quantity of the daily snack. We typically plan for one item per student, but we can adjust when needed.
  • A large number of students will be away (for example, a field trip) and will not receive a snack. Ideally, this is shared the day before distribution.
  • Your class will be away and you would like snacks sent earlier, set aside, or stored for when the class returns (again, ideally the day before).
  • You’re noticing consistent waste for a particular item and think it might help us adjust future servings.
    We see overall waste, but classroom context helps us understand why.

You do not need to contact us if:

  • A parent has verbally asked to lift an allergy concern. Allergy changes must come through the office or in writing from a guardian.
  • A student has a one-off dislike of a snack.
  • A parent is looking for contact information for the Snack Program. We maintain a separate community-facing page for families.

How to contact us

  • A short reporting form is preferred
  • Email is always OK if the form does not capture what you need

What Is the Student Nutrition Program (SNP)?

The Rawlinson Student Nutrition Program provides a daily snack to every student, regardless of whether a family has submitted an opt-in form.
This is an intentional change from previous years.

The program exists to:

  • Support student wellbeing
  • Ensure equitable access to food
  • Reduce barriers for families

How the program works

  • The SNP is run primarily by volunteers
  • Food is funded through:
    • Toronto Foundation for Student Success (TFSS) — primarily donated, perishable food such as fruit and dairy
    • Healthy Selections — ordered items with more flexibility in portion size
    • Millennium Bakery — baked goods
  • Because much of the food is donated, substitutions are not always possible
  • Menus may change based on:
    • Availability
    • Surplus
    • Spoilage risk

Our goal is consistency and equity, not perfection.

Reporting an Issue

If something comes up, please use the Snack Program Reporting Form whenever possible.
Email is fine if the form does not capture what you need.

You can use the form to report:

  • Food waste or feedback
  • Dietary or allergy concerns
  • Field trips or large numbers of absences

What to expect after submitting

  • You will receive an email confirmation from one of the volunteers
  • We may follow up if clarification is needed
  • Not every submission will result in a visible change, but all are reviewed

Snack Program Calendar

  • This calendar shows planned snack distributions
  • It is informational only and not a commitment
  • Plans may change based on surplus or perishable items that need to be prioritized

Teachers are not required to act on this calendar.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the bags sent to classrooms, and what if my students don’t receive one?

The bags contain dietary substitutions for students who require them.

  • If a student does not receive a bag, their dietary needs do not require a substitution
  • If you are unsure, please check with the office

Should a snack be offered to every student in the class?

Yes. A snack should be offered to every student, regardless of whether an opt-in form has been submitted.

What if my class is away?

If your class will be away (for example, a field trip), please let us know in advance if possible.
We can adjust quantities, send snacks early, or store them for when the class returns.

What if a student has no allergy form on file?

Students do not need to opt in to receive a snack.

If a student has an allergy or dietary concern:

  • Please report it using the form
  • Allergy concerns cannot be lifted without written confirmation from a guardian or the office

Can I request a specific snack?

You are welcome to make requests, but we cannot guarantee specific items.
Availability and funding sources may limit what we can provide.

What criteria are used for snack items?

All items follow:

  • TDSB nutrition guidelines
  • Sugar and sodium limits appropriate for student nutrition programs

Why don’t we serve certain items?

We do not have:

  • A prep kitchen
  • Food handling certifications

For safety reasons, this limits what we can serve.

Who runs the Snack Program?

With the exception of the principal, office staff, and a teacher liaison, the SNP is run entirely by volunteers.
This includes coordination, packing, distribution, and managing dietary needs.
